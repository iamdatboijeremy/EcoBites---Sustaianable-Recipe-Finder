Ogbono Soup

60 mins
140 cal per 100g

A thick and flavorful soup made from wild mango seeds (ogbono), cooked with assorted meats, fish, and vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup Ground ogbono
  • 500g Assorted meats (goat meat, beef, or fish)
  • 2 tablespoons Palm oil
  • 2 Onions, chopped
  • 3 Tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon Ground pepper
  • To taste Salt

Instructions

  1. Boil the assorted meats until tender.
  2. In a separate pot, heat palm oil and sauté onions and tomatoes.
  3. Add ground ogbono, ground crayfish, ground pepper, and salt. Stir to form a thick paste.
  4. Add the boiled meat and stock, then simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or rice.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Ogbono is derived from the wild mango tree, a crop that grows with little intervention and requires minimal water.
  • This dish supports the local economy by using locally harvested meat and fish, reducing transportation emissions.
  • The soup utilizes ingredients with a low environmental impact.
