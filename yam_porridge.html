Yam Porridge

40 mins
189 cal per 100g

A comforting and nutritious dish featuring yam, vegetables, and plant-based seasonings, made sustainably with locally sourced ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-sized (locally sourced if possible) yam
  • 1 tablespoon (sustainably sourced, or substitute with olive oil) palm oil
  • 1, chopped (local and in season) onion
  • 2 cloves, minced garlic
  • 1 small, chopped (or other seasonal vegetables) bell pepper
  • 1-2, chopped (or 1/2 cup canned tomatoes) tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon (dried or fresh if available) thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon (or to taste) ground pepper
  • 1 tablespoon (optional, or substitute with nutritional yeast) ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon (optional) ground ginger
  • 1 cup (homemade or low-sodium) vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup (or other leafy greens like kale) spinach
  • to taste salt
  • 1/4 cup (optional, like parsley or cilantro) fresh herbs
  • 1/4 cup (optional, for crunch and protein) roasted peanuts

Instructions

  1. Peel the yams and cut them into medium cubes. Rinse well and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, add the cubed yam and enough water to cover them. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the yams are soft but still hold their shape. Drain and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, heat the palm oil (or olive oil) over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the bell pepper, tomatoes, thyme, ground ginger, and ground pepper. Stir and cook for about 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes soften and release their juices.
  5. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Cook the vegetable mixture for an additional 5 minutes.
  6. Add the cooked yams to the pot and stir to combine. Let the yam cook in the broth and vegetable mix for about 10 minutes to meld the flavors.
  7. Stir in the spinach, kale, or other leafy greens, cooking for another 2-3 minutes until the greens are wilted.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or additional spices as needed.
  9. Once the porridge yam reaches the desired consistency (thick but not too dry), remove from heat.
  10. Garnish with fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro and sprinkle roasted peanuts on top before serving.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Locally Sourced Yam: Reduces the carbon footprint by using locally grown yams.
  • Seasonal Vegetables: Flexible and encourages the use of seasonal vegetables like spinach, kale, or peppers.
  • Plant-Based: Entirely plant-based, making it eco-friendly with a smaller carbon footprint.
  • Homemade Broth: Reduces packaging waste and minimizes food waste by using vegetable scraps.
  • Minimal Waste: Encourages using leftovers and fresh ingredients to reduce food waste.
  • Energy Efficient: Cooking in one pot and simmering conserves energy during preparation.
