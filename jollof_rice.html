Jollof Rice

45 mins
285 cal per 100g

Jollof Rice is a popular and flavorful West African dish that is typically made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of seasonings. To make it more sustainable, you can focus on using local, seasonal ingredients, minimizing food waste, and incorporating plant-based or low-impact proteins. Below is a sustainable Jollof Rice recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 14 oz (1 can) diced tomatoes
  • 1, chopped onion
  • 1, chopped bell pepper
  • 2 cloves, minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon, minced fresh ginger
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari (optional)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, green beans)
  • to taste salt
  • to taste pepper
  • for garnish fresh cilantro or parsley (optional)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water to remove excess starch. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a blender, combine the diced tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, garlic, and ginger. Blend until smooth.
  3. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the tomato paste and cook for 1-2 minutes. Then, pour in the blended tomato mixture. Stir well and let it cook for 10-15 minutes to thicken.
  4. Stir in the thyme, curry powder, paprika, bay leaf, soy sauce (if using), salt, and pepper. Let simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the rinsed rice and stir to coat in the tomato sauce. Pour in the vegetable broth and stir again. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Cover the pot and reduce heat to low. Cook for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add more broth if needed until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
  7. About 10 minutes before the rice is done, stir in the seasonal vegetables. Cover and let them cook with the rice until tender.
  8. Taste and adjust seasonings if necessary. Remove the bay leaf before serving.
  9. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley, if desired, and serve.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Seasonal and Local Ingredients: By using fresh or local vegetables and making your own vegetable broth, you reduce food miles and packaging waste.
  • Plant-Based: The dish is naturally plant-based, which is generally more sustainable than animal-based alternatives. It also allows you to add more vegetables and legumes for additional protein.
  • Rice: Parboiled rice is often produced sustainably, but choosing a local variety helps reduce environmental impact. If possible, seek out rice brands that support ethical farming practices.
  • Minimal Waste: The use of leftover or seasonal vegetables helps minimize food waste. You can also store any leftover rice for the next day.
