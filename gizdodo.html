Gizdodo

50 mins
253 cal per 100g

Gizdodo is a popular Nigerian dish made with fried plantains and spicy gizzards. The gizzards are typically marinated and fried, then mixed with sautéed onions, peppers, and tomatoes, creating a savory, flavorful dish. The combination of tender gizzards and sweet plantains offers a balance of protein and carbohydrates, often served as a side or main dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-sized chicken breasts (or plant-based alternative like tofu or tempeh)
  • 4 ripe plantains (local and in season)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or sustainably sourced vegetable oil)
  • 1 large onion (chopped, local and in season)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped, or canned if fresh is out of season)
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to spice level)
  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish (optional, or nutritional yeast for a plant-based umami flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or fresh if available)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari (low-sodium for health and flavor)
  • to taste salt
  • 1-2 cups vegetable broth (homemade for reduced waste, or water)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh herbs (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Peel and slice the plantains into round or diagonal pieces. Heat some oil in a frying pan over medium heat and fry the plantain slices until golden brown and crispy on both sides. Once fried, set them aside on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  2. If using chicken, cut it into bite-sized cubes. Heat some oil in a separate frying pan and cook the chicken until golden brown and cooked through. If using tofu or tempeh, cube and sauté in oil until crispy and golden. Set aside.
  3. In the same pan used for the chicken (or separately if preferred), add a tablespoon of oil and sauté the chopped onions and minced garlic for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, ground crayfish (or nutritional yeast), scotch bonnet peppers, ground pepper, and thyme. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to rise to the top. Stir occasionally to prevent burning. If the sauce becomes too thick, add a small amount of vegetable broth or water to loosen it.
  5. Once the sauce is ready, add the cooked chicken (or plant-based protein) to the pan and stir to combine. Let it cook in the sauce for an additional 3-5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Add soy sauce (or tamari) and salt to taste. For a creamier texture, add a little vegetable broth or water.
  6. Add the fried plantain slices into the pan with the chicken and sauce, stirring gently to combine. Let it cook together for another 3-4 minutes to ensure the plantains absorb the sauce, becoming flavorful and coated.
  7. Serve the gizdodo hot, garnished with fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro, if desired.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Plant-Based Protein Alternative: By using tofu or tempeh instead of traditional animal protein, the carbon footprint of the dish is significantly reduced.
  • Local and Seasonal Ingredients: Plantains are locally grown and in-season, making them a more sustainable crop with lower environmental impact.
  • Minimal Waste: Encourages using whole, fresh ingredients and repurposing plantain skins for other uses like making snacks or chips.
  • Reducing Packaging Waste: Fresh plantains and vegetables reduce the reliance on pre-packaged ingredients, and homemade seasoning blends can replace store-bought condiments.
  • Sustainable Oil Use: Opting for sustainably sourced palm oil or alternatives like olive or sunflower oil minimizes environmental harm.
  • Optional Plant-Based Flavor Additions: Substituting nutritional yeast for crayfish or adding smoked paprika enhances flavor while keeping the dish vegan-friendly.
