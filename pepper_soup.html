Pepper Soup

35 mins
60 cal per 100g

Pepper soup is a flavorful and spicy broth-based dish popular in Nigeria and other West African countries. It is typically made with meat (like fish, goat, or chicken) or plant-based proteins, along with a variety of aromatic spices, herbs, and vegetables. The broth is light but highly aromatic, often served as a comforting meal or appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups preferred protein (fish, chicken, or plant-based options like tofu or tempeh)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or sustainably sourced vegetable oil)
  • 1 large onion (chopped, local and in season)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons grated ginger
  • 2-3 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to spice level)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped, or canned if fresh is out of season)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or fresh if available)
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari (low-sodium for health and flavor)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (homemade for reduced waste)
  • to taste salt and pepper
  • 1 tablespoon fresh herbs (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cut your protein into bite-sized pieces. Heat oil in a pot and brown the protein (fish, chicken, tofu, etc.) for a few minutes, then set it aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the chopped onions, garlic, and ginger until softened (3-4 minutes). Add the scotch bonnet peppers and cook for another 2 minutes.
  3. Add the tomatoes, thyme, bay leaves, and vegetable broth to the pot. Bring to a boil and then simmer for 10-15 minutes to let the flavors infuse.
  4. Add the protein back into the pot and simmer for another 10-15 minutes to cook through. Season with soy sauce (or tamari), salt, and pepper to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve hot. You can pair it with yam, plantains, or bread for a complete meal.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Local and Seasonal Ingredients: Pepper soup often uses locally grown spices, herbs, and vegetables like peppers, onions, and yams. Sourcing these ingredients locally and seasonally reduces transportation costs and supports local farming practices.
  • Minimal Waste: Vegetable scraps (such as pepper stems or onion skins) can be used to make homemade vegetable broth. Leftover soup can be easily stored and eaten later, minimizing food waste.
  • Reducing Packaging Waste: Using fresh ingredients from local markets or farmers’ markets, rather than pre-packaged ones, reduces the amount of single-use plastic packaging. Homemade broths or seasoning can further cut down on packaging waste.
  • Sustainable Oil Use: Choosing oils like sustainably sourced olive oil or coconut oil helps reduce the environmental impact of oil production, especially compared to unsustainably sourced palm oil.
  • Optional Plant-Based Flavor Additions: Nutritional yeast, herbs, and spices can be used as plant-based alternatives to traditional animal-derived seasonings like crayfish, further making the dish vegan-friendly.
