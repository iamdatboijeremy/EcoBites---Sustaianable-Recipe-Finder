Pounded Yam

40 mins
100 cal per 100g

A traditional Nigerian dish made from yam tubers pounded into a smooth, stretchy dough, typically served with soup.

Ingredients

  • 4 Medium-sized yams
  • As needed Water

Instructions

  1. Peel the yams and boil them in water until soft (about 30-40 minutes).
  2. Drain the yams and pound them in a mortar and pestle until smooth and stretchy. Alternatively, use a stand mixer or food processor.
  3. Serve immediately with a variety of soups or stews.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Yams are a drought-tolerant crop that grows well in the semi-arid regions of Northern Nigeria.
  • They require minimal irrigation compared to water-intensive crops like rice.
  • Since yams are grown locally, they support small-scale farmers and have a lower environmental footprint.
