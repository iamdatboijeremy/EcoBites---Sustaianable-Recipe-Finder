Bitter leaf Soup

35 mins
80 cal per 100g

Bitterleaf soup is a rich and flavorful soup from Nigeria, made with bitterleaf, a local leafy vegetable, along with meat, fish, and seasonings. The bitterness of the leaves is balanced by the rich, hearty broth made with ingredients like palm oil and ground spices. It is often served with pounded yam, fufu, or rice and is a favorite dish for special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups bitterleaf (fresh or dried, sustainably sourced)
  • 2 cups plant-based protein (tofu, tempeh, or mushrooms) or meat
  • 1 tablespoon palm oil (sustainably sourced)
  • 1 large, chopped onion
  • 2-3 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish (optional)
  • 1 cup vegetable broth (homemade)
  • to taste salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Bitterleaf: Wash and rinse the bitterleaf thoroughly to reduce the bitterness (if using fresh).
  2. Cook the Protein: Cook the protein of your choice until tender. Set aside.
  3. Make the Soup: Heat palm oil in a pot, sauté onions and peppers, and then add the ground crayfish. Add vegetable broth and bring to a simmer.
  4. Combine and Cook: Add the protein and bitterleaf to the soup and cook for another 10 minutes.
  5. Serve: Serve with pounded yam, rice, or another starch of your choice.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Local and Seasonal Ingredients: The soup relies on locally sourced, seasonal vegetables such as bitterleaf, pumpkin leaves, and other greens, supporting local farmers and reducing transportation emissions.
  • Minimal Waste: The soup is made with fresh, whole ingredients, and vegetable scraps can be used for broths or other dishes. Leftovers can also be stored for later, helping to reduce food waste.
  • Reducing Packaging Waste: Using fresh, whole ingredients instead of pre-packaged items reduces packaging waste. Homemade broths and spices further minimize the need for store-bought products, which often come in plastic packaging.
  • Sustainable Oil Use: Using sustainably sourced oils like olive or vegetable oil for the soup minimizes the environmental impacts associated with oil production.
  • Optional Plant-Based Flavor Additions: To make the soup vegan, plant-based flavorings like nutritional yeast or miso can be used instead of animal-derived ingredients like fish or stock cubes, reducing the environmental impact of the dish.
