Akara

35 mins
220 cal per 100g

Akara is crunchy on the outside and soft on the inside, making it a savory, satisfying treat. It’s an iconic dish in Nigerian and West African cuisine and is often served with a spicy sauce or as a filling accompaniment to other meals.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried black-eyed peas (or any preferred beans)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or sustainably sourced vegetable oil)
  • 1 large onion (chopped, local and in season)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to spice level)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric (optional, for color)
  • to taste salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder (for fluffiness)
  • 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast (or ground crayfish, optional)
  • 1-2 cups water (for blending)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley or cilantro (optional, for garnish)
  • 1-2 tablespoons olive oil (for brushing, optional)
  • a pinch ground pepper or paprika (optional, for additional flavor)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the dried black-eyed peas thoroughly. Soak them in water for 4-6 hours or overnight to soften.
  2. After soaking, rub the beans between your hands or with a clean cloth to remove the skins. Rinse the beans after peeling.
  3. In a blender or food processor, combine the soaked and peeled beans, chopped onion, minced garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers. Add water gradually to achieve a thick paste.
  4. If using cumin, turmeric, or nutritional yeast/ground crayfish, add them to the blender along with salt to taste. Blend until smooth.
  5. Add baking powder to the mixture and stir well to incorporate.
  6. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) or prepare a large frying pan if frying the akara.
  7. Form small balls or patties from the bean mixture using your hands or a spoon and place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet or in the frying pan.
  8. For baking: Brush the akara patties lightly with olive oil and bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  9. For frying: Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat and fry the akara patties in batches until golden brown and crispy on both sides. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  10. Serve warm with a side of spicy tomato sauce or dipping sauce of your choice. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro if desired.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Plant-Based Protein: Beans are nutrient-dense and provide essential fiber and protein without the heavy carbon footprint of animal protein.
  • Local and Seasonal Ingredients: Using locally grown beans, onions, and peppers reduces transportation emissions and supports local farmers.
  • Minimal Waste: Encourages making vegetable broth from kitchen scraps and storing leftover akara for future use, minimizing waste.
  • Alternative Cooking Methods: Baking instead of frying reduces oil usage, making the recipe healthier and more eco-friendly.
  • Sustainable Oil Use: Using olive oil or sustainably sourced vegetable oil helps minimize environmental harm.
  • Reducing Packaging Waste: Using dried beans instead of canned beans reduces packaging waste, especially if bought in bulk.
  • Optional Plant-Based Umami: Nutritional yeast provides a plant-based alternative to ground crayfish, keeping the dish vegan-friendly.
