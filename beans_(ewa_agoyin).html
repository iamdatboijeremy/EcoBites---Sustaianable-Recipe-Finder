Beans (Ewa Agoyin)

120 mins
347 cal per 100g

A sustainable version of the classic Nigerian mashed beans dish, paired with a spicy, flavorful sauce and optional plantains.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (locally grown and in season) dried black-eyed peas or preferred beans
  • 1 tablespoon (sustainably sourced, or olive oil as an alternative) palm oil
  • 1 large, chopped (local and in season) onion
  • 2-3 cloves, minced garlic
  • 2 medium, chopped (or canned if fresh is unavailable) tomatoes
  • 1-2 (adjust to spice level) scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 tablespoon (optional, or nutritional yeast for umami flavor) ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon (or to taste) ground pepper
  • 1 teaspoon (or fresh if available) dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon (low-sodium) soy sauce or tamari
  • to taste salt
  • 2-3 medium-sized (optional, for a side dish) plantains
  • 1-2 cups (homemade for reduced waste, or water) vegetable broth
  • optional, for garnish fresh herbs

Instructions

  1. Rinse the beans thoroughly and cook in a pot of water for 30-40 minutes until tender. Drain and set aside. If using canned beans, drain and rinse before use.
  2. In a frying pan, heat palm oil (or olive oil) over medium heat. Sauté onions and garlic for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes, ground crayfish (or nutritional yeast), scotch bonnet peppers, ground pepper, and thyme. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the sauce thickens and oil separates. Add vegetable broth or water to loosen if necessary.
  4. Mash the cooked beans to your preferred consistency, either smooth or chunky.
  5. Add the mashed beans to the sauce and stir to combine. Cook for 5 minutes to allow flavors to meld. Stir in soy sauce and salt to taste, adding broth or water for a creamier texture if desired.
  6. If serving with plantains, peel and slice them. Fry in a separate pan with oil until golden and crispy. Drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  7. Serve the Ewa Agoyin with fried plantains or bread, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Plant-Based Protein: Beans provide a nutrient-dense, plant-based protein source with a lower environmental impact than animal proteins.
  • Local and Seasonal Ingredients: Emphasizes local, seasonal produce like beans, onions, and peppers to support local agriculture and reduce food miles.
  • Minimal Waste: Encourages using fresh ingredients and homemade vegetable broth to minimize packaging waste and food waste.
  • Sustainable Palm Oil: Recommends sustainably sourced palm oil to prevent deforestation, with olive oil as an eco-friendly alternative.
  • Reducing Packaging Waste: Uses fresh beans and vegetables to reduce reliance on packaged, processed foods.
  • Optional Sides: Includes locally grown plantains as an optional, low-impact side dish, with baked plantains or other alternatives for variety.
