Eba and Ogbono Soup

1hr and 30mins
Eba - 200 cal per 100g
Ogbono soup - 140 cal per 100g

Eba is a classic Nigerian dish made from garri (cassava flour), typically served with a rich, hearty soup. Ogbono Soup (wild mango seed soup) is a flavorful, thick soup often made with meats, fish, and palm oil. This sustainable version focuses on plant-based ingredients, reducing food waste, and using eco-friendly sources while maintaining its deliciousness.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups garri (cassava flour)
  • 3 cups hot water
  • 2 tablespoons sustainably sourced palm oil or olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped (adjust to spice level)
  • 1 tablespoon ground ogbono (wild mango seeds)
  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish (or nutritional yeast)
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup cooked beans (black-eyed peas, kidney beans, or chickpeas)
  • 1/2 cup tofu or tempeh (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh herbs (parsley or cilantro, optional) for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring 3 cups of water to a boil in a medium-sized pot.
  2. Gradually add the garri to the boiling water, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  3. Stir until smooth and thick. Adjust water as needed for preferred consistency.
  4. Mold the eba into a smooth, round shape and set aside.
  5. In a large pot, heat the sustainably sourced palm oil (or olive oil) over medium heat.
  6. Add chopped onions and minced garlic, sautéing for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  7. Add chopped tomatoes, scotch bonnet peppers, ground ogbono, ground crayfish (or nutritional yeast), ground pepper, and thyme. Cook for 5-7 minutes until fragrant.
  8. Stir occasionally, adding a small amount of vegetable broth if needed to prevent burning.
  9. Add cooked beans and tofu (or tempeh), stirring well.
  10. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing flavors to blend.
  11. Add salt to taste and adjust consistency with more broth or water if needed.
  12. Serve the Ogbono soup with the Eba, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.
