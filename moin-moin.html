Moin-Moin

35 mins
200 cal per 100g

Moin Moin is a steamed Nigerian dish made from blended beans, peppers, onions, and spices, then wrapped in banana leaves or steamed in a mold. It is a protein-packed dish and is often served as a side or accompaniment to rice or other dishes. Moin Moin can be made with additional fillings like eggs or fish for extra flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups black-eyed beans (locally grown)
  • 1 large, chopped onion
  • 2-3 (adjust spice level) scotch bonnet peppers
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (or sustainably sourced vegetable oil)
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish (optional)
  • 1 cup vegetable broth (homemade)

Instructions

  1. Soak the beans overnight. Peel them and blend with onions, peppers, and a little water until smooth.
  2. Add the blended bean mixture to a bowl, and stir in thyme, ground crayfish, and vegetable broth to get a thick batter.
  3. Pour the mixture into leaves or containers and steam for 45 minutes to an hour.
  4. Serve with rice or as a standalone meal.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Local and Seasonal Ingredients: The use of local ingredients like peppers, onions, and tomatoes ensures the dish is seasonal and supports local agriculture, reducing the carbon footprint of transportation.
  • Minimal Waste: Leftover Moin Moin can be stored for later meals, helping to minimize food waste. Additionally, vegetable scraps can be composted or used in homemade broth.
  • Reducing Packaging Waste: By purchasing dry beans and fresh produce, you avoid the waste from canned or processed ingredients. Moin Moin can also be made with minimal packaging by using leaves or reusable containers for steaming.
  • Sustainable Oil Use: Opting for sustainably sourced vegetable oil, such as sunflower oil, helps reduce environmental damage associated with unsustainable oil production.
  • Optional Plant-Based Flavor Additions: Using plant-based flavorings such as nutritional yeast, smoked paprika, or mushrooms can enhance the flavor profile while making the dish fully vegan and reducing animal product use.
