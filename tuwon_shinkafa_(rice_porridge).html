Tuwon Shinkafa (Rice Porridge)

30 mins
143 cal per 100g

A thick, creamy porridge made from rice, commonly served for breakfast or as a side dish with soups and stews.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Rice (preferably locally grown)
  • 5 cups Water (filtered)
  • 1/4 cup Vegetable oil
  • to taste Salt
  • 1 tablespoon Ground pepper (optional)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly to remove excess starch and impurities.
  2. In a pot, add rice and water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer, stirring occasionally, for 15–20 minutes, or until the rice becomes tender and the water is absorbed.
  3. Add the vegetable oil and salt, stirring to combine. For a spicier option, add ground pepper.
  4. Cook for an additional 5 minutes until the porridge thickens to a creamy consistency.
  5. Serve the porridge hot with your choice of soup or stew.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Rice is grown in many parts of Northern Nigeria, and using locally grown rice for Tuwon Shinkafa supports local rice farming communities.
  • By focusing on traditional, minimal-processing cooking methods, this dish avoids the need for highly processed ingredients that often have a larger environmental footprint.
  • The rice production process also uses less water than other grains like wheat, making it a more water-efficient choice.
