Okro Soup

65 mins
160 cal per 100g

A sustainable take on the classic Nigerian dish, featuring plant-based proteins, seasonal okra, and eco-friendly practices.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (locally grown and in season) fresh okra
  • 2 tablespoons (sustainably sourced, or olive oil as an alternative) palm oil
  • 1, chopped (local and in season) onion
  • 2 cloves, minced garlic
  • 1 small, chopped (or seasonal peppers) bell pepper
  • 2, blended (or canned if fresh is unavailable) tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon (optional, or substitute with nutritional yeast) ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon (or to taste) ground pepper
  • 1 teaspoon (or fresh if available) dried thyme
  • 2 cups (preferably homemade) vegetable broth
  • 1 cup (black-eyed peas, kidney beans, or chickpeas) cooked beans
  • 1/2 cup (optional, for added protein) tofu or tempeh
  • to taste salt
  • 1/4 cup (parsley or cilantro, optional, for garnish) fresh herbs
  • 1/4 cup (optional, for added richness) roasted peanuts or groundnut paste

Instructions

  1. Wash the okra thoroughly. Slice or chop the okra into small pieces, or blend lightly for a smoother texture.
  2. Heat palm oil (or olive oil) in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté onions and garlic for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  3. Add chopped bell pepper and cook for 2 minutes. Stir in blended tomatoes, ground crayfish (or nutritional yeast), ground pepper, and thyme. Cook for 5-7 minutes until thick and fragrant.
  4. Add cooked beans and tofu (or tempeh) to the pot. Stir and cook for 5-7 minutes to incorporate flavors.
  5. Pour in the vegetable broth, bring to a simmer, and cook for 10 minutes to blend flavors.
  6. Add chopped okra and cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing it to thicken the soup naturally. Adjust the consistency by adding more okra or cooking longer.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or additional broth. Stir in roasted peanuts or groundnut paste if desired for extra richness.
  8. Serve hot with your preferred side dish, such as rice, fufu, pounded yam, or boiled plantains.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Plant-Based Protein: Beans, tofu, or tempeh replace meat, reducing the environmental footprint.
  • Local and Seasonal Ingredients: Emphasizes local, seasonal produce like okra and tomatoes to reduce food miles.
  • Homemade Broth: Encourages broth made from kitchen scraps to reduce waste and packaging.
  • Sustainable Palm Oil: Suggests sustainably sourced palm oil or olive oil to protect biodiversity.
  • Waste Reduction: Promotes repurposing leftovers and scraps, reducing food and packaging waste.
  • Minimal Packaging: Focuses on fresh ingredients to reduce reliance on packaged goods.
