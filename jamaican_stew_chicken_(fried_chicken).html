Jamaican Stew Chicken (Fried Chicken)

90 mins
250 cal per 100g

A flavorful chicken dish made with a blend of spices, onions, tomatoes, and bell peppers, commonly served with rice or plantains.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (preferably free-range or locally farmed)
  • 1 large, finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves, minced garlic
  • 1, chopped green bell pepper
  • 2, chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 tablespoon thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric (optional)
  • to taste salt
  • to taste pepper
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil (or palm oil for added richness)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. Clean and cut the chicken into pieces. Season it with salt, pepper, curry powder, thyme, turmeric, and soy sauce. Marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large frying pan over medium heat.
  3. Fry the seasoned chicken pieces until golden brown, about 10-15 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same pan, sauté onions, garlic, and green bell peppers for 5 minutes until softened.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, ground pepper, curry powder, and other spices. Cook for another 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes break down.
  6. Return the fried chicken to the pan and mix it with the sauce, letting it simmer for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  7. Serve the stew chicken with rice or yam.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Using locally farmed or free-range chicken ensures that the meat comes from smaller operations that typically have a smaller carbon footprint than industrial chicken farms. These small-scale farms are more likely to employ natural farming practices and contribute to regional economies.
  • The use of locally grown vegetables and spices further reduces transportation emissions and supports local agricultural systems.
  • By focusing on natural seasonings and avoiding processed additives, this dish maintains a low environmental impact.
