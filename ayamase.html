Ayamase

35 mins
319 cal per 100g

Ayamase is a rich and flavorful Nigerian green pepper sauce made from green bell peppers, onions, and spices. It is typically cooked with meat or fish but can also be made with plant-based proteins. This version focuses on sustainability by using locally sourced ingredients and reducing food waste.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-sized green bell peppers
  • 2 (adjust to spice level) scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1, chopped onion
  • 1 tablespoon palm oil (sustainably sourced)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish (optional)
  • to taste salt
  • 1 cup vegetable broth (homemade)

Instructions

  1. Blend the green bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, and onions into a smooth paste.
  2. In a pan, heat the palm oil. Add the blended pepper mixture and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste, ground crayfish (if using), and salt. Cook for another 5 minutes to deepen the flavors.
  4. Pour in the vegetable broth, stir, and let the sauce simmer for 10 more minutes until thickened.
  5. Serve hot with rice, yam, or your choice of starch.

Your Notes

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Seasonal and Local Ingredients: Using locally grown green bell peppers, onions, and locust beans (iru) reduces transportation emissions and supports local farmers.
  • Minimal Waste: Utilizing the entire vegetable and storing leftover sauce for later use minimizes food waste.
  • Reducing Packaging Waste: Choosing fresh produce over pre-packaged alternatives helps reduce single-use plastic packaging.
  • Sustainable Oil Use: Using responsibly sourced palm oil or alternatives like sunflower oil reduces environmental impact.
  • Plant-Based Options: Replacing animal-based seasonings with nutritional yeast or smoked paprika makes the dish fully plant-based and more sustainable.
